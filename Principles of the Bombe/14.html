Assumptions and Implications

We will now make two two guesses about how the Enigma machine was set up when the crib was encoded.

We will then assume that these guesses are correct, even though they are very likely to be wrong. We therefore call these two guesses our assumptions.

Unless you are familiar with mathematical proof by contradiction, then making these assumptions may seem like a very confusing thing to do, but it will become clear soon enough why making these assumptions is useful.

Our first guess is to arbitrarily choose some initial scrambler position.

Our second guess is to arbitrarily choose a steckering for one of the letters in the menu. In this case, we will guess that a is steckered to b, that is, the stecker variable α takes on the value b.

Remember that we now assume that both of these guesses are correct, even though, in reality, this is likely not the case.
