Increasing Feedback

The idea was the brain child of Gordon Welchman, and the logic goes as follows:

You should now be familiar with the idea that saying "a is steckered to b" is completely synonymous to saying "b is steckered to a". That is, we cannot have one without the other, since they both refer to the same setting on the Enigma machine (the presence of a stecker cable between the a and b sockets in the plugboard).

We also know by now, that when the b wire of the α cable is powered this means that α = b. We also know that this represents the assumption or conclusion that "a is steckered to b"

Furthermore, when the a wire of the β cable is powered this means that β = a. This represents the assumption or conclusion that "b is steckered to a"

But we just reasoned that "b is steckered to a" and "b is steckered to a" are synonymous. So for a valid steckering we will never have the b wire of the α cable being powered without the a wire of the β cable also being powered.

The grand idea, therefore, is to hard-wire an electrical connection between the b wire of the α cable, and the a wire of the β cable. We will add connections between all such equivalent pairs of wires which represent the same steckering.

The wires we will add are collectively referred to as the diagonal board. We will see over the page why it was given this name...
