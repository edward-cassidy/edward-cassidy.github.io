The Plugboard

There are two main components which perform the encryption: the scramblers, and the plugboard.

The plugboard (Figure 2.2) consists of 26 pairs of sockets, each labelled with a letter. There are also 10 stecker cables, where each end can be plugged into a socket pair in the plugboard. When setting up the Enigma machine, the operator will plug in all 10 of these stecker cables into some configuration of sockets, so that only six socket pairs are left unconnected to a stecker cable.

When a stecker cable is plugged in between the socket pairs labelled a and b, we say that a and bare steckered, or a is steckered to b, or vice-versa. When no stecker cable is connected to a, we say that a is unsteckered, or a is self-steckered.
