Operation: The Scramblers

All of the 26 a to z wires that exit from the plugboard arrive on the right-hand side of the scramblers. Each of these wires makes contact with a different metal contact on right side of the fast scrambler.

Electrical current then passes through each scrambler in turn from right to left until it reaches the reflector.

Recall that the reflector only has metal contacts on the right-hand side, which are paired in some arbitrary configuration. This means that when current enters the reflector on its right-hand side, it comes back out on the same side, but from a different metal contact. Current then travels back through the scramblers, but this time from left to right.

Electrical current emerges from some wire on the right-hand side of the scramblers, although a necessarily different wire from the input to the scramblers. The input letter has therefore been scrambled by the scramblers.

Note that as the scramblers rotate with each keypress, pressing the same key multiple times in a row will yield different outputs for each keypress from the Enigma machine.
