Adding The Diagonal Board

In using the diagram, hopefully you noticed that in every cable, every wire except for one is connected to a diagonal wire. Why is this? Let's look at the γ (gamma, c's associated stecker variable) cable as an example.

The a wire of the γ cable is hard-wired to the c wire of the α cable, since both of these wires represent the hypothesis that a is steckered to c.

The b wire of the γ cable is hard-wired to the c wire of the β cable, since both of these wires represent the hypothesis that b is steckered to c.

The c wire of the γ cable is hard-wired to... nothing?

This can be explained by considering the hypothesis that the wire represents. Powering of the c wire of the γ cable represents the hypothesis that c is steckered to c, that is, c is unsteckered (or self-steckered).

All such wire's which aren't connected to the diagonal board are all those which represent a self-stecker hypothesis. Such cases do not give us any information about other stecker variables, hence why they lack a connection to the diagonal board.
