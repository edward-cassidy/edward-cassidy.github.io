Making Effective Crib Predictions

One aid is through intelligence about trends in German messages.

For example, the British operators may have known that the Germans would send a weather report every morning at a particular time. Therefore, they could guess that the first messages of the day would contain “weather report” (Wetterbericht in German).

This was further aided by a crucial error in the design of Enigma machines: it could not encode a letter to itself.

This can be exploited to more easily and accurately determine the position of the crib in the ciphertext, as we will see in the next page.
