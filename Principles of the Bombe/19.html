Assumptions and Implications

This process can continue, but the overall idea is as follows:

Firstly we assume an initial scrambler position.

Secondly we assume the value of a single stecker variable in the menu.

Finally, based on these assumptions, we can conclude the value of any other stecker variable in the menu, given we know which scramblers were used, and have a working copy of them to perform scrambling operations.

We still haven't shown why making assumptions, which are most likely incorrect, and deriving conclusions from them is at all useful. This is the topic of the next slides.
