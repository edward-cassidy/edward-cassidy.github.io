Setup

When setting up the Enigma machine, the operator must select three scramblers to use from a choice of five (which are aptly named scramblers I, II, III, IV, and V). Each of these five 'standard' scramblers has different internal wiring, although every 'I' scrambler has the same internal wiring as every other 'I' scrambler that is produced, and so on. The operator must also choose an order in which to insert these three scramblers.

The operator must also select a reflector from a choice of two: reflector B or reflector C (although a fourth reflector D was also used towards the end of WW2). The reflector is inserted to the left of the three chosen scramblers, with the metal contacts facing the left-most scrambler.

When inserted, the contacts on adjacent scramblers and the reflector rub against each other. Critically, the scramblers (but not the reflector) can be each independently rotated to any of 26 positions.

Before typing a message, the operator must rotate the scramblers to some initial position. The edges of the scramblers are labelled with letters from A to Z, so the desired initial scrambler position is specified by a three-letter code, such as ABC. (Technically the operator sets the ring settings before the initial rotor positions, but that is beyond the scope of this recap).
