The Scramblers

The scramblers are somewhat more complicated than the plugboard.

A scrambler by itself is a disk that looks rather like a cog. On both flat sides of the scrambler, there is a ring of 26 metal contacts (see Figure 2.3). Each metal contact on one side is electronically connected to exactly one metal contact on the other side. Around the circumference are written the letters of the alphabet, each which lines up with a different contact.

Fundamentally, the contacts are not connected in a straight line through the scrambler, rather they are scrambled up based on a mess of internal wiring.
