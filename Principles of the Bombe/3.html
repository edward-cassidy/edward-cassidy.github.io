The Problem at Hand

Firstly, we want to determine the absolute initial scrambler positions, that is, the actual position of the scramblers before the crib was encoded, not the relative positions.

For a real Enigma machine where each scrambler has 26 positions, there are 17576 possible initial scrambler positions the Enigma operator can choose from.

However, we may not know which scramblers were used by the Enigma.

Remember that M3 Enigma had a total of 5 different scramblers to choose from (labelled by roman numerals), as well as a choice of two reflectors (ignoring reflector D). The Bombe can only try one combination of scramblers at a time, so the Bombe run may need to be repeated up to 120 times before the correct scrambler order is found, or we realise that our guess for the crib was incorrect!

Hopefully, however, British intelligence will already have some idea which scramblers were used, so the number of scrambler orders to try is often significantly fewer than 120.
