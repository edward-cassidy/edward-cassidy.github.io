Operation: Input and Scrambler Rotation

Now that the plugboard and scramblers are set up to their desired positions, the Enigma operator can encode a message. (although actual protocol for sending a message was more complicated than this).

When a key is pressed on the Enigma machine, say the a key, the first thing that happens is the scrambler on the far-right rotates by one step (so if it was previously in position A, it is now in position B).

For every full rotation the far-right scrambler makes, the middle scrambler will rotate by one, and similarly for the far-left scrambler (although not exactly, as the true mechanism for the far-left scrambler's rotation is slightly more complicated and unintuitive). Due to how often each scrambler rotates, the far-right scrambler is often called the fast rotor, and the far-left the slow rotor.

It should be noted that the next scrambler doesn't necessarily rotate when the previous scrambler rotates from Z back to A. In fact, the timing of these turnovers is what the ring settings define (which, again, are out of the scope of this recap).
