Non-Contradictions

It therefore turns out that the fact our menu has a cycle in it was vital to ruling out a possible steckering.

That being said, if we were to instead travel around the cycle and find again that α = b (so there is no contradiction), then this still doesn't mean our assumptions are correct.

The only thing we can say is that we cannot prove the assumptions are incorrect.

It is also worth noting that our choice to make assumptions about the stecker variable α was completely arbitrary. For example, we could have instead made an assumption about the value of ζ, and then used the loop between ζ and β.
